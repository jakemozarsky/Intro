Ohio State wins Cotton Bowl, advances to National Championship

By Jake Mozarsky

Ohio State might have lost "The Game" to rival Michigan, but since, the Buckeyes have been unstoppable. This once again was the case Friday night in Dallas, where they defeated Texas 28-14 in the Cotton Bowl.

Concerns surrounded Ohio State entering the College Football Playoff after a 13-10 loss to the Wolverines that saw them miss out on the Big Ten Championship. The No. 1 Buckeyes have went on a dominating run in the postseason, beating No. 9 Tennessee 42-17, No. 1 Oregon 41-21 and now defeating the No. 5 Longhorns.

Source: KSL Sports

The Buckeye defense, who entered the game No. 1 in yards per game and points per game allowed, stopped Texas on a goal line stand with two minutes remaining. On fourth down, senior defensive end Jack Sawyer sacked Texas quarterback Quinn Ewers and returned it 83 yards for a touchdown, icing the game.

With the win, Ohio State advances to its first National Championship since 2021 looking to win their first title since 2014. The Buckeyes face No. 7 Notre Dame in Atlanta on January 20.
